Why InstaVid?
The lowdown on why we built this app first
Feb 27, 2014

So why did we build InstaVid? Turns out there's actually an answer to that question. Before we started thinking of any app ideas, Skyler and I laid out some guiding principles to get our heads in the right mindset.

  • No dev budget
  • 6 month dev cycle (max)
  • Easy marketing potential

Sounds impossible, right? That's what we thought too -- but we knew that we had to stick to these guidelines if we wanted a shot at getting into the hardware realm with no outside funding. The clear solution was this, so we went for it.

We tossed around a few different ideas, and then Skyler had the brilliant realization that there was no easy way to post videos that weren't shot on your device to Instagram. He owns a GoPro camera and as any GoPro user knows; there's no easy way to upload your GoPro clips to Instagram.

This initial idea was enough to get the ball rolling, and we actually started out calling the app InstaRoll. That was its sole purpose -- to allow people to easily save videos from outside sources to their CameraRoll, so they could then be uploaded to Instagram.

After a week or so of pursuing this idea, it became clear that we were on to something much bigger. We realized there was a large audience of people that could really benefit from having a more robust video editing app for Instagram.

We of course knew that we would also befefit from the app too. I wholeheartedly agree with Phil Libin that it's important to build something for you, that way you never become bored of the idea you're pursuing.

We're now a couple of weeks away from submitting the app to the AppStore and couldn't be more excited about it. We have plans to port it to Android and also make it available in as many languages as possible.

We really hope InstaVid allows people around the world to express themselves in more creative ways and as always, let us know your thoughts in the comments below!
